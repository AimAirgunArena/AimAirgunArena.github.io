About Airguns

What are airguns?

Air guns, as opposed to firearms, function by utilising compressed air to propel a projectile forward. In India, air pistols and rifles shooting projectiles that are .177 inch (4.5mm) in diameter, can be owned legally, without a weapons’ license.

How do they work?

"Air guns are of different types, each with a different mechanism."

Then there are pneumatic air guns with an on board pump. So when it is cocked, the pump compresses the air and upon pulling the trigger, this compressed air propels the projectile. These pneumatic air guns either have a single stroke pump or a multi-stroke pump, with the latter offering the option of higher powered shots for varying scenarios.

There are also pre-charged pneumatic (PCP) air guns. These have a cylinder on board, which is filled with an external compressor. Each trigger pull releases a specific amount of compressed air, that shoots the projectile out. These are usually the most expensive ones and are the ones used in shooting competitions. These also offer the most consistency for every shot. Some of the most powerful air guns are PCPs.

There are also guns that use a cartridge of compressed CO2. These are called CO2 guns and their functioning is similar to that of a (PCP) but instead of a cylinder which has to be filled, these use disposable CO2 cartridges.
